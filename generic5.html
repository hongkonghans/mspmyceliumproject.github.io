Ganoderma

Ganoderma is a large genus of brown-fleshed polypores with a disting. lacquered cap crust that is meltable. Some species are annual, relatively pale-fleshed, soft and have stems (this page spread); others are perennial, dark-fleshed, hard, and broadly double-walled, brown, ornamented attached valeg, B70% All have unique, spores, and are white-rotters.

Ganoderma includes both, pale-fleshed annual polypores with + stipitate fruitbodies (page 840) and broadly adnate, dark-fleshed perennial polypores. The double-walled, brown, ornamented spores are mostly easily detectable by rubbing a wet finger on the cap surface. White rotters.
