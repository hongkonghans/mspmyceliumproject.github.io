Trametes versicolor

Trametes versicolor is a tough perennial polypore that looks thin-and-pale-fleshed, felty, and densely zonate, which contains nearly black, narrow, smooth zones with a silky sheen. There are 4-5 spherical pores per millimeter. The hyaline spores are 5-6.52-2.5 micrometer in size.

Trametes versicolor can be found on all types of deciduous wood, most commonly on stumps, and very rarely on conifers. Fagus is by far the most prominent host in the nemoral zones.

The primary monosaccharide components are glucose, mannose, and a trace of galactose, xylose, and fucose (Anhui, 2003)

Biological characteristics, pharmacological action and application prospect of Coriolus versicolor J Anhui Agric Sci, 31 (3) (2003), pp. 509-510
