Fomes fomentarius

Fomes fomentarius is a polypore which is considered a tough perennial. It usually forms into a hoof-shape as it ages, hence it is also occasionally called the hoof fungus. It can be discovered on both fallen and stranding hardwoods. The upper gray zones on the fungus are representative of its wood-like surface, while the brown pore surface has small round pores. If one was to slice open the hoof-fungus, its vaguely layered tubes will be revealed. The hoof fungus’ fruiting body usually appears as a single specimen however, occasionally two or more tiers are produced.

You can expect to find the hoof fungus in northern mainland Europe as well as Scotland, with higher rarity in southern Britain and Ireland.

Its yearly tube layers build up together to produce a large hoof-shaped structure; 10-40cm across and up to 20cm deep at the core of its attachment line. After first couple of years, brackets are said to cincease in thickness gradually, but don’t generally increase ein diameter; thus resuliting in said hoof shape.
