Hypholoma

Hypholoma, the genus name, means 'mushrooms with threads'. It may be a reference to the thread-like partial veil that connects the cap rim to the stem of young fruitbodies, although some authorities suggest that it is a reference to the thread-like rhizomorphs (root-like bundles of mycelial hyphae) that radiate from the stem base.

Hypholoma is a widespread genus found throughout the world, and its most well-known species typically grow on dead wood. However, there are also some species that can be found growing in moss, including those found on moors.

IThe fruiting bodies of Hypholoma mushrooms typically have a central stipe (stem). They come in various sizes, with some being medium-sized and others smaller. The caps of these mushrooms can range in color from yellow/brown to brick red, and they have a smooth surface.

One characteristic of Hypholoma mushrooms is that the developing mushroom is initially enveloped in a veil, but this feature is only visible in very young specimens. As a result, there is no well-defined ring (annulus) around the stripe, and the cap does not have distinctive flakes. However, there may be cottony ring zones on the stipe, which can sometimes be colored by the dark spores.
