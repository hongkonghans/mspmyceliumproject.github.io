Pleurotus ostreatus

Pleurotus ostreatus has a broad, fan-shaped or oyster-shaped cap that spans 2-30 centimetres. The colour ranges from white to grey or tan to dark brown; the edge is inrolled when young, smooth, and frequently somewhat lobed or wavy. Because of the stipe structure, the flesh is white, solid, and varies in thickness.

Pleurotus ostreatusâ€™ gills are white to cream in colour and descend on the stem if present. If this is the case, the stipe is asymmetrical, with a lateral connection to wood. The mushroom's spore print is white to lilac-gray and best seen against a black backdrop. The stipe of the mushroom is frequently lacking. It is short and dense when present. It has a bittersweet scent of benzaldehyde. (Beltran-Garcia et al, 1997)

The oyster mushroom is found in many temperate and subtropical woods across the world, although it is not found in the Pacific Northwest of North America, where P. pulmonarius and P. populinus have taken its place. (Trudell & Ammirati, 2009)

It is a saprotroph that decomposes wood as a main decomposer, particularly deciduous trees and beech trees. It is a fungus that causes white rot in wood. (Phillips, 2006)
